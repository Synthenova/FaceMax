## What It Is
Bone smashing (also called "bone hitting" or "bone remodeling") is an extreme looksmaxxing technique that involves striking facial bones with blunt objects to cause micro-fractures, theoretically triggering bone remodeling and increased bone density in targeted areas. It has gained attention through influencers like Clavicular but is widely criticized by medical professionals.

## The Theory (Unproven)
Proponents claim that controlled micro-trauma to bone stimulates osteoblast activity, causing:
- Increased bone density
- Bone remodeling in targeted areas
- Wider jaw or more prominent cheekbones

**Reality:** There is NO peer-reviewed scientific evidence supporting these claims in the context of facial bone smashing. Wolff's Law (bone adapts to stress) applies to gradual, physiological loads - not traumatic impact.

## What Medical Professionals Say

**Orthopedic Surgeons:** Facial bones are not designed for impact trauma. Repeated striking causes:
- Stress fractures
- Compartment syndrome
- Compromised blood supply to bone
- Irregular healing patterns

**Dermatologists:** External trauma damages:
- Skin tissue
- Subcutaneous fat
- Blood vessels
- Nerves
- Connective tissue

**Neurologists:** Facial region contains:
- Multiple cranial nerves
- Delicate sinus structures
- Risk of traumatic neuropathy

## Reported Injuries from Bone Smashing
- Permanent facial asymmetry
- Chronic facial pain
- Loss of sensation
