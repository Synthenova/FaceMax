# Scapular Retraction

## Overview
- **Category**: Posture & Upper Body
- **Target Areas**: Rhomboids, Middle trapezius, Lower trapezius, Postural muscles
- **Equipment Needed**: None (optional: resistance band)

## How It Works
Scapular retraction involves pulling the shoulder blades together and down the back. This exercise counteracts the forward shoulder posture common from desk work and phone use. Proper scapular positioning creates a broader upper frame, opens the chest, and indirectly improves facial aesthetics by aligning the head properly over the shoulders, which enhances jawline visibility.

## Instructions

### Step-by-Step (All Levels)
1. **Starting position** - Stand or sit with spine neutral, arms at sides
2. **Relax shoulders** - Let them drop away from ears
3. **Squeeze shoulder blades** - Pull them together and DOWN (not up)
4. **Imagine crushing** a pencil between your shoulder blades
5. **Hold contraction** while breathing normally
6. **Release slowly** - Don't let shoulders roll forward

### Beginner Variation
- **Duration per session**: 3-5 minutes
- **Frequency**: Daily, multiple times
- **Intensity**: Gentle engagement, focus on awareness
- **Specific modifications**:
  - Perform seated or standing
  - Hold contraction for 5 seconds
  - 10-12 repetitions
  - Focus on the "down and back" motion
  - Can do while working at desk
  - Set hourly reminders

### Intermediate Variation
- **Duration per session**: 5-8 minutes
- **Frequency**: Daily, 2-3 sessions
- **Intensity**: Moderate contraction with full engagement
- **Specific modifications**:
  - Add resistance band (light to medium)
  - Hold for 8-10 seconds
  - 15-20 repetitions
  - 2-3 sets
  - Combine with chin tucks
  - Practice during breaks

### Advanced Variation
- **Duration per session**: 10 minutes
- **Frequency**: Daily, integrated into full posture routine
- **Intensity**: Strong contraction with integrated movements
- **Specific modifications**:
  - Use stronger resistance band
  - Wall slides with retraction hold
  - Prone Y-T-W exercises
  - Face pulls with band
  - Hold for 10-15 seconds
  - 20-25 repetitions, 3 sets
  - Combine with full posture correction protocol

## Common Mistakes
- **Shrugging shoulders up** - Should move BACK and DOWN, not up
- **Over-squeezing** - Can cause muscle spasms
- **Holding breath** - Breathe normally throughout
- **Rushing** - Slow, controlled movements are key
- **Arching lower back** - Keep core engaged and neutral
- **Only squeezing** - Need to RELEASE fully between reps

## Safety & Disclaimers

### Risk Level: VERY LOW

### Who Should Avoid
- Recent shoulder surgery (without clearance)
- Active shoulder injuries
- Severe kyphosis (consult physical therapist first)

### Warning Signs to Stop
- Shoulder pain (not muscle fatigue)
- Numbness in arms
- Sharp or shooting pain

### Potential Side Effects
- Mild muscle soreness between shoulder blades (normal)
- Temporary fatigue

### Medical Disclaimer
> **IMPORTANT:** This exercise is generally safe for most people. Consult a healthcare professional if you have:
> - Chronic shoulder or upper back conditions
> - Recent upper body surgery
> - Persistent pain during the exercise
>
> Proper form is essential. If unsure, consult a physical therapist.
