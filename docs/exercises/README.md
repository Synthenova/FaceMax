## Safety Overview

### Risk Level Distribution

| Risk Level | Exercise Count | Examples |
|------------|----------------|----------|
| **Very Low** | 30 exercises | Fish Face, Neck Rolls, Face Tapping, Scapular Retraction |
| **Low** | 12 exercises | Mewing, Gua Sha, Posture, Platysma Pushes, Deep Smile |
| **Moderate** | 6 exercises | Mastic Gum, Hard Mewing, Jawzrsize, Neck Training |
| **High** | 0 exercises | Bone smashing [documented but NOT recommended](./bonesmashing.md) |

### Universal Safety Guidelines

1. **Start Slow** - Begin with beginner variations even if you're experienced
2. **Listen to Your Body** - Discomfort is okay; pain means stop
3. **Consistency Over Intensity** - Daily gentle practice beats sporadic intense sessions
4. **Proper Form** - Quality of movement matters more than quantity
5. **Rest Days** - Muscles need recovery time, even facial muscles

### When to Stop Immediately
- Sharp pain in jaw, neck, or face
- Dizziness or nausea
- Clicking/popping in jaw joint (TMJ warning)
- Numbness or tingling
- Headaches that persist
- Skin irritation or rash

### Medical Consultation Recommended
- TMJ disorder history
- Recent facial surgery
- Dental issues or braces
- Cervical spine problems
- Skin conditions
- Any chronic medical conditions
