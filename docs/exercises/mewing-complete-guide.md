# Mewing - Complete Guide

## Overview
Mewing is a technique focused on maintaining proper tongue posture against the roof of the mouth. Named after Dr. John Mew, this practice aims to potentially improve jawline definition, facial symmetry, and overall facial aesthetics through consistent oral posture correction.

**Category:** Oral Posture / Facial Structure
**Difficulty:** Beginner to Advanced
**Time to Results:** 6-24 months (long-term practice)

---

## The Science Behind Mewing

### How It Works
The theory behind mewing suggests that proper tongue posture exerts gentle, consistent pressure on the maxilla (upper jaw), potentially influencing:
- Forward growth of the midface
- Widening of the dental arch
- Improved airway patency
- Better facial symmetry
- More defined jawline

### Evidence Level
**Mixed/Anecdotal.** While many practitioners report positive changes, peer-reviewed scientific studies on mewing's effectiveness for adults are limited. The technique is based on established orthotropic principles but individual results vary significantly.

## Soft Mewing (Recommended for Beginners)

### Proper Technique

#### Step 1: Lip Seal
- Gently close your mouth so your lips are together
- No forceful pressing - just light contact
- Breathe through your nose exclusively

#### Step 2: Teeth Position
- Allow upper and lower teeth to touch gently OR remain slightly apart (2-3mm)
- **Do NOT clench** - this causes TMJ issues and enamel wear
- Teeth should be in light, passive contact

#### Step 3: Full Tongue Placement
This is the critical component:
- **Tip of tongue:** Rest just behind your upper front teeth (not touching them)
- **Middle of tongue:** Flattened against the hard palate
- **Back third:** Pressed up against the soft palate

#### The "NG" Trick (Finding Proper Back Tongue Position)
1. Say the word "sing" or "thing" and hold the "ng" sound
2. Notice where the back of your tongue touches
3. This is exactly where your back tongue should rest during mewing
4. Practice until this position becomes natural

#### Step 4: Body Posture
- Maintain straight back and neck alignment
- Forward head posture makes proper mewing difficult
- Imagine a string pulling the top of your head upward

### Duration
- **Goal:** Make this your default resting tongue position
- **Practice:** All day, every day (subconscious habit)
- **Consistency:** More important than intensity

## Hard Mewing (Advanced - Use Caution)

### What Is Hard Mewing?
Hard mewing involves applying **active, forceful pressure** with the tongue against the palate, rather than passive resting.

### Technique
- Assume the soft mewing position
- Actively push upward with significant force using your tongue muscles
- Hold the pressure consistently

### ⚠️ Important Warning
**Many practitioners and oral health experts advise against hard mewing because:**
- Excessive force can cause TMJ pain
- May lead to headaches
- Can cause unwanted tooth movement
- Risk of developing improper form

**Recommendation:** Soft mewing maintained for long durations is considered safer and potentially more effective than intense but inconsistent hard mewing.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### 1. Touching Front Teeth
- **Problem:** Pushing tongue against front teeth can push them forward
- **Result:** Gaps between teeth (diastema), protrusion
- **Fix:** Tongue tip should be near, but not touching, upper incisors

### 2. Forgetting the Back Third
- **Problem:** Only lifting the front of the tongue
- **Result:** Incomplete technique, limited benefits
- **Fix:** Use the "NG" trick to engage the posterior tongue

### 3. Teeth Clenching
- **Problem:** Biting down hard while mewing
- **Result:** Jaw pain, enamel erosion, TMJ issues
- **Fix:** Teeth should touch lightly or not at all

### 4. Mouth Breathing
- **Problem:** Can't maintain tongue posture while mouth breathing
- **Result:** Defeats the purpose of mewing
- **Fix:** Address nasal congestion; practice nasal breathing exclusively

### 5. Improper Neck Posture
- **Problem:** Slouching or forward head posture
- **Result:** Restricts airway, makes proper tongue position difficult
- **Fix:** Maintain neutral spine alignment

## Progression Timeline

### Weeks 1-2: Awareness Phase
- Tongue will feel tired and sore
- Constant conscious effort required
- Difficulty maintaining position while talking/eating
- May feel awkward or unnatural

### Weeks 3-4: Adaptation Phase
- Muscle memory begins developing
- Can hold position for longer periods
- Start to notice when tongue drops
- Less conscious effort needed

### Months 2-3: Habit Formation
- Position becomes more natural
- Can maintain during most daily activities
- Tongue strength improves
- May begin to feel "wrong" when not mewing

### Months 6-12: Subconscious Habit
- Mewing becomes default resting position
- Minimal conscious effort required
- Tongue posture maintained during sleep (if sleep position allows)
- Potential visible changes may begin

## Supporting Exercises

### 1. Chin Tucks (McKenzie Chin Tuck)
- Helps correct forward head posture
- Improves airway and makes mewing easier
- See separate exercise guide

### 2. Tongue Chewing
- Strengthens tongue muscles
- Practice proper chewing with tongue on palate
- Use sugar-free gum

### 3. Swallowing Practice
- Proper swallow: Tongue pushes up and back, not against teeth
- Practice water swallows with correct tongue posture
- Essential for preventing relapse

## Troubleshooting

### My tongue gets tired/sore
**Normal.** Tongue muscles are weak from disuse. Consistency will build strength.

### I can't breathe while mewing
- Check back tongue position (may be too far back)
- Ensure nasal passages are clear
- Start with soft mewing
- Consider consulting an ENT if persistent

### No visible changes after months
- Verify technique is correct (all three thirds of tongue up)
- Results take 6-24 months minimum
- Individual anatomy varies significantly
- Document with photos for comparison

### Jaw pain while mewing
- Likely clenching teeth or pushing too hard
- Switch to soft mewing immediately
- Consult dentist/TMJ specialist if pain persists

## Safety & Disclaimers

### Risk Level: LOW

### Who Should Avoid
- Individuals with severe TMJ disorder
- Those with recent oral surgery (consult surgeon)
- People with severe sleep apnea (consult sleep specialist first)
- Anyone experiencing significant pain

### Warning Signs to Stop
- Persistent jaw pain or clicking
- Tooth movement or gap formation
- Chronic headaches
- Difficulty swallowing
- Sleep disruption

### Potential Side Effects
- Temporary tongue soreness (normal)
- Mild jaw fatigue (normal in early stages)
- Excessive force can cause TMJ issues
- Improper technique may affect teeth alignment

### Medical Disclaimer
> **IMPORTANT:** Mewing is a technique popularized through social media and orthotropic communities. While based on established principles of oral posture, scientific evidence for dramatic structural changes in adults is limited.
>
> Consult a healthcare professional before starting, especially if you have:
> - TMJ disorder or jaw pain
> - Sleep apnea or breathing difficulties
> - Recent dental work or orthodontic appliances
> - Any concerns about oral health
>
> This information is for educational purposes only and is not medical advice. Individual results vary significantly.
